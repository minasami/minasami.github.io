A leadership point of view

Digital health is not an IT installation

It is a transformation of decisions, responsibilities, information flow and patient or workforce experience. The technology matters, but the operating model determines whether the product becomes part of care—or becomes another unused system.

1

Start with the care or service journey

Map the people, decisions, delays, exceptions, safety concerns and handoffs before choosing features.

2

Design governance with the workflow

Define clinical ownership, product ownership, escalation paths, decision rights, data responsibility and change control.

3

Measure adoption and outcomes

A go-live date is a milestone, not the outcome. Measure sustained use, turnaround, quality, safety and value.

Delivery framework

A practical operating model for digital-health projects

The strongest programs combine disciplined project controls with clinical and operational empathy.

1. Frame the problem

  • Define the healthcare, operational and business problem.
  • Identify who experiences the problem and who owns the outcome.
  • Create a baseline using turnaround, quality, cost, access or experience measures.

2. Map the real workflow

  • Document the normal path and important exceptions.
  • Separate clinical decisions from administrative approvals.
  • Identify duplication, waiting, rework and information gaps.

3. Establish governance

  • Create an accountable sponsor, product owner and clinical owner.
  • Define stage gates, risks, escalation, privacy and change control.
  • Use executive reporting that highlights decisions—not activity noise.

4. Build and validate iteratively

  • Prototype high-risk workflows early with end users.
  • Validate usability, data quality, role permissions and exception handling.
  • Keep requirements traceable to outcomes and risks.

5. Prepare the organization

  • Build role-based training, SOPs, support and escalation routes.
  • Engage champions and address resistance with evidence and feedback.
  • Plan migration and parallel operations deliberately.

6. Sustain value

  • Monitor adoption, service performance, incidents and user feedback.
  • Run benefits reviews after launch.
  • Prioritize improvements through clinical value and operational impact.
For CEOs and healthcare leaders

Questions that reveal whether a program is ready

Strategic value

  • Which measurable healthcare or operational outcome will change?
  • What happens if we do nothing?
  • Does this initiative simplify the organization or add another layer?

Ownership and safety

  • Who owns the clinical outcome and who owns the product?
  • Which decisions can the system support, and which must remain human?
  • How will exceptions, incidents and unintended consequences be handled?

Adoption

  • How does the new workflow fit daily work?
  • What will users stop doing after implementation?
  • Who is accountable for adoption after the project team leaves?

Evidence

  • What baseline exists today?
  • Which leading and lagging indicators will be reviewed?
  • When will leadership decide to scale, redesign or stop?
