How to digitize a healthcare support workflow

By Mina Samy Tawfik Saad

Digitization fails when teams automate a broken process. The first step is to understand the real operating model: who submits the request, who verifies documents, who makes clinical decisions, who approves exceptions, who prepares medicines, and who owns the final outcome.

Core principle: design the workflow before selecting the tool.

1. Define stages and ownership

Every request should have a clear stage, a named owner and a visible next action. Without this, work becomes dependent on memory and informal follow-up.

2. Separate clinical and operational decisions

Clinical review, administrative eligibility, pharmacy preparation and delivery coordination are different responsibilities. The system should reflect these differences rather than forcing every team into one generic queue.

3. Make escalation visible

Delayed documents, unavailable medicines, unclear eligibility and urgent clinical needs require structured escalation routes. Escalation should be tracked, not handled only through private messages.

4. Build reporting into the workflow

Dashboards should be produced from the operating process itself. Useful metrics include request volume, turnaround time, pending cases, fulfillment status, recurring medicine demand and budget utilization.

5. Treat training as part of implementation

A technically correct system can still fail if users do not understand why the process changed. Training, SOPs, role guides and early feedback loops are essential parts of delivery.

The strongest digital-health systems do more than store data. They create clarity, accountability and continuity across the entire service journey.
