Implementation playbook

Days 1-30: Align and design

  • Confirm sponsor and decision rights.
  • Review readiness result and Decision Journal.
  • Map current workflow, exceptions and handoffs.
  • Name clinical, operational, technical and adoption owners.
  • Define baseline KPIs and success criteria.
  • Create risk register and first governance cadence.

Days 31-60: Build and pilot

  • Translate workflow into operating model and system requirements.
  • Prepare role-based training and support routes.
  • Run controlled pilot or repair sprint.
  • Track adoption risks and operational issues weekly.
  • Validate reporting definitions and data quality.
  • Escalate blockers through the sponsor route.

Days 61-90: Review and decide

  • Compare pilot evidence against success criteria.
  • Review adoption, workflow, data and benefits signals.
  • Decide whether to scale, repair, redesign or stop.
  • Update Decision Journal with evidence and assumptions.
  • Define next-stage governance and resourcing.
  • Convert lessons learned into case-study evidence.
Governance gates

Stage gates that protect the initiative

Gate 1: Ready to pilot?Sponsor, workflow, owners, risks, KPIs and adoption plan are clear enough to test.
Gate 2: Ready to scale?Pilot evidence shows adoption, operational fit, data trust and measurable value.
Gate 3: Needs repair?Weak dimensions are specific, owned and repairable within a defined sprint.
Gate 4: Needs redesign or stop?The operating model, ownership or value case is too weak to justify continued scale.
