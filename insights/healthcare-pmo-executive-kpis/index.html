Executive measurement

PMO value is decision quality

A healthcare PMO should not be a reporting factory. It should create visibility that helps leaders protect patients, employees, budgets, timelines and strategic outcomes.

1. Delivery health

  • Milestone status and slippage trend.
  • Critical path risks.
  • Dependency readiness.
  • Decision aging: how long key decisions remain unresolved.

2. Workflow adoption

  • Active users by role.
  • Completion rate for the intended workflow.
  • Parallel-process signals, such as manual workarounds or duplicated tracking.
  • Training completion and post-training confidence.

3. Service performance

  • Turnaround time from request to closure.
  • Escalation volume and root causes.
  • Rework rate and missing-data rate.
  • Backlog age and bottleneck owner.

4. Risk and quality

  • Open high-risk issues.
  • Incident trends after launch.
  • Data-quality exceptions.
  • Clinical, privacy or operational risk exposure.

5. Value realization

  • Benefits achieved versus baseline.
  • Cost or time saved where measurable.
  • Experience improvement for users, patients or employees.
  • Sustainment ownership after project closure.

The dashboard test

If a dashboard does not help a leader decide, escalate, remove a blocker or change priority, it is probably decoration. The best healthcare PMO metrics connect project activity to operational reality.
