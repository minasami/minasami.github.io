Audit areas

Five checks before adding more pages

1. Claim accuracy

Every strong claim should match the Claims Registry or have a clear evidence page.

Check Claims Registry →

2. Evidence path

A recruiter or executive should reach proof within one click from any major positioning page.

Check Proof Room →

3. Navigation clarity

Every page should have one obvious next step. Too many equal CTAs weaken action.

Check Command Center →

4. Conversion discipline

Pages should route to a conversation, assessment, proof asset, or outreach action.

5. Growth usefulness

6. Freshness

Risk controls

Protect the brand from common failure modes

Do not overclaim

Avoid phrases that imply sole budget ownership, completed credentials, or unverified enterprise certifications.

Do not scatter attention

Use one best landing page for each audience instead of sending the entire website.

Do not create pages without routes

Every new page needs an entry point, next action, and reason to exist.

Do not confuse audiences

CEO pages, recruiter pages, PMO pages, and operations pages should speak to different needs.

Monthly rhythm

Run this audit once per month

Week 1

Review claims, credentials, contact details and evidence links.

Week 2

Review analytics and identify the top three pages by useful attention.

Week 3

Improve one weak page: headline, CTA, proof, or navigation.

Week 4

Publish one insight or LinkedIn post based on the strongest signal.

Rule

Growth without governance becomes noise

The website should keep getting stronger, but not by adding random pages. Add pages only when they improve proof, distribution, conversion, decision-making, or trust.
