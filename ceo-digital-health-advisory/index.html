What leadership must solve

The hidden reasons healthcare technology underperforms

Technology-first decisions

A tool is purchased before the care journey, service journey and operating model are understood.

Unclear ownership

No single accountable owner connects clinical value, operations, data, implementation and adoption.

Weak change management

Training, SOPs, support, incentives and escalation routes are treated as afterthoughts.

Bad measurement

Leadership tracks deployment activity instead of adoption, turnaround, quality, safety and experience.

Disconnected teams

Clinicians, operations, IT, vendors, finance and leadership work with different definitions of success.

No sustainment model

After go-live, there is no disciplined improvement loop for feedback, incidents, usage and benefits.

CEO diagnostic

Five questions before funding another healthcare technology project

  1. What exact workflow will change? Name the people, decisions, handoffs and exceptions.
  2. Who owns the clinical or operational outcome? Not only the system, but the result.
  3. What will users stop doing? If nothing is removed, the project may add burden.
  4. What baseline will prove improvement? Turnaround, access, quality, safety, experience or cost.
  5. What happens after go-live? Define feedback, support, incidents, adoption tracking and improvement ownership.
