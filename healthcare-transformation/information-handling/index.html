Information Handling in Digital Health

Good information handling defines what data is collected, why it is needed, who may use it and how it is maintained throughout its lifecycle.

Core principles

  • Purpose clarity
  • Minimum necessary data
  • Role-based access
  • Retention and deletion
  • User transparency
  • Exception review

Practical steps

  1. Map information flows.
  2. Identify sensitive data.
  3. Define access needs.
  4. Remove unnecessary collection.
  5. Review retention.
  6. Monitor exceptions.
